EXO Magazine Layout

Architectural Publication Design

Project

The brief for this project was to conceptualize and execute an issue of a publication centered around a chosen theme. The EXO publication was designed with a goal of using type and image to convey the form and philosophy of brutalist architecture, specifically in the realm of the design of parking garages. These structures serve as the physical skeletal structure of our society; often thought to be undesigned and eternal. This publication attempts to demonstrate their inherent aesthetic value.

Process

The process for this project was a progression from rough sketches to digital sketches to final digital spreads. Ultimately, the final deliverable was a cover, a table of contents page, and a series of spreads that sufficiently demonstrate the ideas of the subject. The goal for the final publication was finding a good middle ground between legible and experimental typography. The final was a demonstration of the negotiation betweeen readable type and type that conveyed a sense of the ideology, philosophy, and form of the brutalist architecture that was the subject of the publication.
