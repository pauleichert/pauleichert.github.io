Data Visualization

Informational Design

Project

The goal for this project was to produce three distinct data visualizations depicting a data set — in this case, the various dimensions of similar pairs of pants. The final three compositions use form, shape, color, and scale to depict the different data points. These visualizations shouldn't just be well designed; rather, they should show the data in the best possible way both on a macro and micro scale. The project topic of "pants dimensions" has some real physicality to it. As a result, the compositions have a real tactile feeling to them, with elements precisely correlating to the different dimensions.

Process

The process for this project followed the flow of paper explorations to digital sketches to final compositions. The workflow was informed by the idea of prioritizing communication. Even the paper sketches required some consideration for how the specific data points would be portrayed both from afar and up close. Moving into the digital sketches a big challenge was figuring out how to best show the variance between dimensions of pairs of pants without dwelling too close to the esoteric realm of design. The final compositions are assisted in their communication by alluding to the idea of pants, either literally with photograph or symbolically with forms that reflect the different curves and angles.
