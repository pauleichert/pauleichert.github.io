Animal with Type

Animal with Type was an exercise in combining organic animal forms with typographic exploration. The fundamental composition of the project adhered to a golden ratio grid, with design elements either aligning to that or to each other. The purpose of this project was to gain a greater understanding of how graphical systems can increase the overall aesthetic value of a composition. In this particular case I aligned the composition to both the golden ratio and a 238° angle. In the preliminary stages of this project I constructed a small set of square and rectangular compositions. The primary purpose of each of these studies was a demonstration of the inherent forces within the composition.

The project began with the construction of an animal form. I created the shrimp form out of intersecting circular and eliptical elements. The shrimp was chosen because of the inherent significance of its spiralled form, which aligns rather cleverly to the golden ratio. To accompany the animal subject a sans-serif typeface was required; in this case, Source Sans Variable was used. I chose this particular typeface because I believed it to compliment the simplistic beauty of the previously constructed animal form.
