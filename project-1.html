Animal with Type

Animal with Type is an exercise in combining organic animal forms with typographic exploration. The fundamental composition of the project adhered to a golden ratio grid, with design elements either aligning to that or to each other. The purpose of this project was to gain a greater understanding of how graphical systems can increase the overall aesthetic value of a composition. In this particular case the composition fell along both the golden ratio and a 238° angle.

The project began with the construction of an animal form. This ‘shrimp’ form was created out of the unification of circular and elliptical elements. The 'shrimp' was chosen because its body naturally resembles a spiral, which aligns inherently with the golden ratio. To accompany the animal subject a typeface was chosen; in this case, Source Sans Variable was used. This decision was made to compliment the inherent curvature in the composition.
